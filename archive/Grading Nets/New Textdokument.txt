<b>Grading Nets</b>
Complex Modelling: Inflated Restraint Workshop 
Tutors: Petras Vestartas, Phil Ayres
Students: Katre Laura, Olga Smolnikava, Jonas Mortensen, Mandeep Singh, Teodor Petrov
4-17 March 2016


<b>Context & Aims</b>
This is the first in a series of three workshops that tracks and invites your participation in the research progress and production of Complex Modelling project #5 – Inflated Restraint. The aim of this workshop is to prototype, test, explore and evaluate methods of designing and making graded braid nets for inflatable membranes. The workshop will equip you with tools and methods for designing, calibrating and prototyping across three distinct scales – micro (material)/meso (component)/macro (structure) – with the aim of determining design dependencies across these scales.

<b>Workshop Methods</b>
The workshop combines presentations of project concepts and design tools together with hands-on design exploration using custom digital tools, model calibration techniques and physical prototyping. Empirical testing methods will provide data for model calibration, and calibrated models will be used to provide projective understandings of hybrid material system behaviors. These projections will then be tested and evaluated through comparison with physical prototypes. Continual documentation of activities will act as an essential method of design ideation, communication and planning.
